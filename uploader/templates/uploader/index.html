How does it work?

There are lots of educational resource sites, so what makes this one different? The primary aim is for resources to be aimed at specific syllabuses. The most important part is that resources are linked and rated per syllabus. This means that the resource you upload on fractions might be well rated for a GCSE syllabus but not an A-Level one. The programming resource you upload might be excellent for one exam board and get great reviews but not great for another which will be reflected in the rating.

Why?

Two reasons: 1) resources tailored to specific syllabuses are of higher quality and 2) resources can be useful for multiple subjects but the rating of the resource doesn't reflect the suitability for all syllabuses.
